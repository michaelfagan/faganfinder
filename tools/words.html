These sites generally include profanity.

Green’s Dictionary of Slang is the most comprehensive slang dictionary, and includes historical and modern slang, from all around the English-speaking world. Paid subscribers have additional search options. Their timelines are another way to browse. All other sites here include only modern slang.

The Online Slang Dictionary results include categories that show related words. Urban Dictionary is based on user contributions and many of the terms may be considered offensive.

No Slang.com, NetLingo, SlangIt, and Slang.org include chat and texting slang, especially abbreviations, as well as emoticons. SlangIt is unique in that it does not contain profanity.

The British Slang Dictionary & Translator and A Dictionary of Slang (which has no search) are both focused on slang in the United Kingdom.

Hatebase is a database of hate speech (in any language) and usage intended to prevent violence.

There are a variety of products that can identify and remove profanity from text, although none of them are perfect. Pingar’s profanity check has a free demo that does not require signing up.

The origin of words and phrases is known as etymology.
